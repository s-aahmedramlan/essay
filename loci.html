Letter of Continued Interest (LOCI)

Understanding and crafting effective waitlist letters

What is a LOCI?

A Letter of Continued Interest (LOCI) is a document you send to a college or university after being placed on their waitlist. It's your opportunity to demonstrate that you remain highly interested in attending the school and to provide updates on your achievements, activities, or circumstances since submitting your application.

Why LOCIs Matter

Being waitlisted isn't a rejection—it means the admissions committee saw potential in your application but couldn't offer you a spot in the initial round. A well-crafted LOCI can:

  • ✓ Demonstrate continued interest: Show the school that you're still enthusiastic about attending
  • ✓ Provide updates: Share new achievements, awards, or experiences that strengthen your application
  • ✓ Reinforce fit: Remind the committee why you're a perfect match for their institution
  • ✓ Stand out: Many waitlisted students don't send LOCIs, so doing so shows initiative and commitment

Key Components of an Effective LOCI

1. Professional Opening

Start with a respectful, professional greeting that acknowledges your waitlist status. Express gratitude for being considered and continued interest in the school.

2. Meaningful Updates

Share significant developments since your application:

  • • New academic achievements or awards
  • • Leadership roles or community service
  • • Improved test scores or grades
  • • Relevant experiences or projects

3. Reinforced Interest

Explain why this school remains your top choice. Reference specific programs, professors, opportunities, or aspects of the school that align with your goals. Be specific and genuine.

4. Professional Closing

End with a respectful closing that reiterates your interest and willingness to provide any additional information. Thank the admissions committee for their continued consideration.

Timing is Critical

Send your LOCI within 2-4 weeks of receiving your waitlist notification. This shows promptness and continued interest. If the school has specific instructions or deadlines for waitlist materials, follow them exactly. Some schools may also request additional materials or updates through their portal.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • ✗ Being too casual: Maintain a professional, respectful tone throughout
  • ✗ Repeating your application: Focus on new information and updates
  • ✗ Being desperate or negative: Stay positive and confident
  • ✗ Making it too long: Keep it concise—typically 1-2 pages, single-spaced
  • ✗ Ignoring school-specific instructions: Follow any guidelines the school provides
